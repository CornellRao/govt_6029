Overview and Class Goals

This course continues the graduate sequence in quantitative political methodology, focused particularly on fitting, interpreting, and refining the linear regression model.

Our agenda includes gaining familiarity with statistical programming via the popular R environment, developing clear and informative graphical representations of regression results, and understanding regression models in matrix form.

Prerequisites

It is desirable for students to have taken the introductory course in the sequence (Governemnt 6019), but any prior course on basic social statistics and linear regression should suffice.

Assessment and Evaluation

Problem Sets

Problem sets assigned weekly or bi-weekly. These problem sets will include programming problems with an emphasis on writing understandable, reproducible code.

The assignments and due dates will be distributed during the semester

Assignments will be submitted digitally through blackboard andgithub at the due date.

Poster Presentation

You will use all (relevant) techniques learned in this class to finalize your research project and present your findings to your classmates in poster presetation session. We will invite other facuslty, our chance to show off!

Final Paper

A 15–20 page original report on an original quantitative analysis or replication-and-extension of a published article. The quantitative analysis should be conducted in R and reproducible.

The final paper is due on May 14, 2015 11:59 pm EST.

Further details on the projects will be provided as due dates approach

Email & Blackboard and GitHub:

The teaching team will send announcements regularly by email.

Any non-personal questions related to the material in the course should be posted as a Canvas discussion.

It is often more efficient to answer questions in person, so try to ask them in class or attend office hours.

All of your assignments will be submitted through GitHub and Blackboard. We have no control over your Github submissions but they will have a time stamp and will be public. On the other hand, only you and the teaching team can have access to your Blackboard submission but these will have a due date and once submitted you cannot modify it. For a number of reason that will become evident you will be submitting your code on Github one day after you submit on Blackboard
